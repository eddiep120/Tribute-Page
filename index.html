A Tribute to Pual Konerko

World Series Champion

Paul Konerko hits a grand slam in the 2005 World Series

The following is a chronicling of Paul Konerko's career:

  • He was drafted 13th overall by the LA Dogers in the 1994 MLB draft.
  • He played for the Albuquerque Dukes in Tripple-A and won two Dodgers "Minor League Player of the Year" awards.
  • He made it to the majors for the first time in 97', playing part of the 97' and 98' seasons.
  • He was traded during the 98' season to the Cincinnati Reds, where he played 26 games to finish the season.
  • The Chicago White Sox acquired Konerko from the reds in the off season in a trade for Mike Cameron.
  • Konerko made his first postseason appearance during the 2000 season.
  • He and the Sox returned to the postseason 5 years later where the Sox would win their first World Series since 1917.
  • The most iconic moment of his career came during the 7th inning when he hit a grand slam off of relief pitcher Chad Qualls.
  • Konerko went on to play with the Sox until he retired on September 24, 2014.
  • Konerko's number, 14, was retired by the White Sox organization in 2015.
  • Konerko ranks 2nd on the White Sox all time Home Run Totals, and 44th all time in the Majors.

"Paul Konerko is one of the greatest players in White Sox history, not only for his strength and performance on the field, but also for his heart and leadership off the field." -Jerry Reinsdorf
